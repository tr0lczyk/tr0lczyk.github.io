Privacy Policy — Light Sword

Last updated: 15 July 2026

Thank you for using Light Sword (the “App”). This policy explains what data the App does and does not handle, and what choices you have.

Summary

  • The App shows ads served by Google AdMob. The ads SDK collects a limited set of data (such as your device’s advertising ID) to serve and measure ads. This is the only data that leaves your device.
  • Everything you do in the App — colors, hilt builds, scores, achievements — is stored locally on your device and never uploaded. There are no accounts and no analytics.
  • The camera, motion sensors, flashlight and two-phone duel all work on-device or device-to-device, with no servers involved.

Advertising (Google AdMob)

The App displays banner ads and optional rewarded ads served by Google AdMob (Google Mobile Ads SDK). To serve and measure ads, Google may collect:

  • your device’s advertising ID,
  • coarse, IP-derived information (such as approximate location at the country/region level),
  • basic device and diagnostic information used for ad delivery and fraud prevention.

Google acts as an independent controller of this data. You can read how Google uses information from apps that use its services here:
https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites

Rewarded ads are always optional. Watching one to unlock a blade color, a hilt part or a training continue is your choice; every unlock also has a play-to-earn path.

Your consent choices (EEA / UK)

If you are in the European Economic Area or the UK, the App shows a consent form (Google’s User Messaging Platform) on first launch. You can change or withdraw your choices at any time in the App: saber selection screen → ⓘ (Credits) → Privacy options. You can also reset your advertising ID or opt out of ads personalization in your device’s Google settings.

Data stored on your device only

The App keeps your preferences and progress (chosen blade color, hilt configuration, blade voice, scene, play statistics, achievements, unlocks, training scores) in local storage on your device. This data:

  • never leaves your device,
  • is not linked to any account or identity,
  • is deleted when you uninstall the App.

Device features the App uses

  • Motion sensors (accelerometer, gravity): detect swings, clashes and tilt so the App can play matching sounds and drive the training game. Readings are processed in real time, on device, and are never stored or transmitted.
  • Camera (optional): the saber screen has a camera mode that uses a live preview as the backdrop. The camera image is displayed only — nothing is captured, recorded, stored or transmitted. The App works fully without camera permission.
  • Flashlight: brief LED pulses accompany ignition and clashes (can be turned off in the Hilt Workshop). This uses the flash hardware only.
  • Vibration: haptic feedback on clashes and hits.

Two-phone duel (Nearby Connections)

The optional Duel mode connects two phones directly over Bluetooth / Wi-Fi Direct using Google’s Nearby Connections — no internet, no servers. During a duel, your device shares with the other phone only: your device’s model name (shown as your player name) and real-time game events (swing timing, scores). Nothing is stored or sent anywhere else. Bluetooth and (on older Android versions) location permissions are requested solely because Android requires them for local device discovery — the App never reads or derives your location.

Permissions

PermissionWhy

Children’s privacy

The App is a general-audience toy and does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children. The only third-party processing is ad serving by Google AdMob, subject to the consent controls described above. If you believe a child has provided personal data through the App, contact us and we will help.

Data sharing and selling

We do not sell personal data. We share nothing with third parties beyond the ad-serving data handled by Google AdMob described above.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at the same URL with a new “Last updated” date.
